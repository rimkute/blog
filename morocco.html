    Packing list

    For cold nights: thick jacket, wool socks for sleeping, long underwear/thermal layers, long pants and long-sleeve shirts, scarf or shawl (doubles as lap blanket), light gloves for early morning. For daytime: lightweight breathable clothing, long sleeves for sun protection, comfortable walking/hiking boots, leggings or joggers.
    Accessories: wide-brimmed hat, UV-protection sunglasses, neck gaiter or buff for sand protection, headscarf (also provided by tours). Practical items: power bank (electricity not guaranteed), SPF 50+ sunscreen, lip balm, hand sanitizer/wet wipes, flashlight or headlamp, camera with extra batteries, small backpack—not large suitcase.
    What to leave behind: Large suitcases (bring small backpack only), jeans (too hot and restrictive), shorts alone (need long pants for camel riding), fragile sunglasses (sand scratches easily).
    Camel trekking typically lasts 1-1.5 hours each way, with sunset and sunrise rides being highlights. Travelers advise: "Wear long pants - the camel's fur is scratchy and rough" and "Bring a scarf to protect face from flying sand." Guides show you how to tie traditional Touareg turbans.
    Sandboarding is included at most camps with boards provided. Travelers describe it as "great fun" and "completely different to snowboarding," highly recommending trying it despite it being at your own responsibility.
    Evening entertainment features traditional Berber drumming, nomadic music performances, campfire gatherings, and mint tea service. The atmosphere is "incredibly atmospheric and authentic" with "the sound of traditional drum music filling the air" and "hundreds of candles lit at night around the camp.
    Stargazing in December-January benefits from crystal-clear skies with less dust than other seasons and zero light pollution. Travelers report "the most amazing starlit sky I've ever seen" with "stars so bright you can read by them." Photography enthusiasts should bring a tripod for night shots.
    Motion sickness medication is recommended for the winding mountain roads. Sun protection is essential with the intense desert sun and high altitude exposure.
